Scholes: 'Man Utd defeat is a disgrace'

Dec 6th 2021 4 Min read

Former MU's midfielder Paul Scholes criticized Man Utd for lack of enthusiasm and was overwhelmed in the 0-3 defeat at Sevilla in the second leg of the Europa League quarter-final.

"This is another harsh night, when Man Utd could not cope with the enthusiasm of Sevilla," Scholes said on BT Sport on the evening of April 20. "This defeat is a disgrace. Man Utd couldn't fight and were completely overwhelmed."

Captain Maguire, midfielder Casemrio (left) and goalkeeper De Gea were disappointed after Man Utd conceded the first goal in the 8th minute of the Europa League quarter-final second leg at Sevilla on April 20. Photo: Reuters

After dropping the win at Old Trafford last week, Man Utd continued to make the mistake of being a guest at Sanchez Pizjuan yesterday. In the 8th minute, goalkeeper David de Gea passed to captain Harry Maguire who was being approached by three players right in front of the penalty area. Midfielder Erik Lamela successfully competed, creating conditions for Youssef En-Nesyri to escape and put his left foot in the near corner to open the score for Sevilla.

Early in the second half, the home team doubled the lead thanks to Loic Bade's header. In the 81st minute, De Gea handled the error when rushing out of the penalty area to catch a high ball without pressure, helping En-Nesyri put the ball into the empty goal, complete the double and seal a 3-0 victory. This result caused Man Utd to be eliminated from the Europa League with a total score of 2-5.
According to Scholes, Man Utd needs the experience of the pillars when they have to go to Sevilla - the most successful club in Europa League history with six championships in 2006, 2007, 2014, 2015, 2016 and 2020. However, the former English player was disappointed when De Gea himself made a mistake that made Man Utd soon concede a goal. He also said that Man Utd "shot themselves in the foot" from the first leg, when Tyrell Malacia and Harry Maguire respectively scored to lead to a 2-2 draw.
