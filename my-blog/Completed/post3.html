Mourinho mocks Dutch reporter

Dec 6th 2021 4 Min read

Coach Jose Mourinho gives a cup-shaped keychain to a Dutch reporter after knocking out his compatriot Feyenoord in the Europa League quarter-finals.

In May 2022, Roma beat Feyenoord 1-0 in the Europa Conference League final thanks to Nicolo Zaniolo - midfielder who switched to Galatasaray. This result helped Roma win the first European cup in history and quench their thirst for titles after 14 years, since the Italian Cup in the 2007-2008 season.

The two teams rematch in the quarter-finals of the Europa League this season. In the first leg, Feyenoord won Roma 1-0 in the match in Rotterdam on April 13. After the match, a Dutch reporter asked Mourinho about Roma's chances of turning the game around in the second leg. In familiar ironic style, the Portuguese coach told the reporter to "stop crying" about last year's defeat. "For the past 10 months, I've cried a lot. But I shouldn't cry," said Mourinho. "Because I cried, I couldn't turn defeat into victory. My team lost the game they should have won."

And in the second leg at the Olimpico Stadium on April 20, in the 89th minute the score was 1-1. If maintained, Feyenoord will win the right to advance. But Paulo Dybala scored a late winner to send the game into extra time, where Stephan El Shaarawy and Lorenzo Pellegrini respectively helped Roma win 4-1, thereby advancing to the semi-finals with a total score of 4-2. At the post-match press conference, Mourinho gave the Dutch reporter mentioned on a keychain the image of the Europa Conference League Cup. He said: "I have a gift for you, but don't cry for the next 10 months. Your Feyenoord is leading the Dutch league, playing well so don't cry."

Roma will meet Leverkusen in the semi-finals. The first leg took place in Rome on May 12, and the second leg took place in Germany a week later
